I've decided to officially ditch Windows for good. I'm not settling for WSL, I want the native experience.
I say that, but I am dual-booting, since some games I enjoy (CS2, TFT) rely on anti-cheat software that is only available on Windows.

mistakes/annoyances:
installing ubuntu alongside windows, but NOT alongside the boot manager mucked up my system and i was unable to boot into windows. very annoying.
setting up dual monitors/240hz is a pain. installing nvidia drivers was also not super simple, since i need secure boot enabled.

gaming was a hassle. i went back to fedora especifically because steam was super finnicky and weird on ubuntu (when using i3, my desktop environment of choice)
even still i had to reinstall steam a couple of times inside fedora since i'd enabled gpu acceleration and it makes the whole app go black for some reason?

have not figured out yet how to enable low res (1280x720) 240hz gameplay in CS2 on linux.
